# Natural Portrait Realism Guide

## Purpose

This guide helps reduce common artificial portrait qualities such as plastic skin,
copy-pasted faces, rigid poses, and malformed anatomy.

## Strong portrait direction

Specify:

- Facial reference is for identity, not pose copying
- A new pose and expression for each output
- Real skin texture with pores and subtle tonal variation
- Natural asymmetry
- Environmentally consistent lighting
- Believable hair strands and beard texture
- Correct hands, fingers, ears, and teeth
- Wardrobe that reacts naturally to the pose

## Reusable quality-control language

```text
Preserve recognizable facial identity without copying the reference pose,
expression, hairstyle, or lighting. Create a newly photographed moment.

Maintain natural skin texture, pores, subtle under-eye detail, realistic beard
and hair strands, and normal facial asymmetry. Avoid waxy skin, beauty-filter
smoothing, plastic highlights, over-white eyes or teeth, and pasted facial edges.

Use anatomically correct hands with five natural fingers on each visible hand.
Ensure realistic wrist angles, shoulder structure, clothing tension, and contact
shadows.
```

## Pose variation checklist

Change at least three elements between versions:

- Camera height
- Shoulder angle
- Hand position
- Weight distribution
- Gaze direction
- Expression
- Hairstyle
- Wardrobe silhouette
- Lighting direction
- Background depth

## Review questions

1. Does the face belong to the body and lighting?
2. Is the expression specific rather than generic?
3. Are both hands anatomically correct?
4. Does skin retain texture at normal viewing distance?
5. Is the pose different from the reference?
6. Do clothing folds correspond to body movement?
