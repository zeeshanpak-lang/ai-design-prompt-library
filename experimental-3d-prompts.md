# Experimental 3D Prompt Collection

## 1. Sculptural Brand Object

```text
Create an experimental 3D campaign visual for [BRAND OR PRODUCT].

Creative idea:
Transform the product into a sculptural object that feels engineered, collectible,
and visually unfamiliar while still remaining recognizable.

Composition:
Use one dominant form with asymmetrical balance, controlled negative space,
and a strong relationship between the object and typography.

Materials:
Combine [MATERIAL 1], [MATERIAL 2], and [MATERIAL 3] with realistic thickness,
surface variation, reflections, and physical contact.

Lighting:
Use directional studio lighting with one strong highlight, deep controlled shadows,
and subtle environmental bounce.

Typography:
Integrate the headline into the spatial composition. Let the type pass behind,
through, or around the object without becoming unreadable.

Quality controls:
No generic chrome blob, no random floating shapes, no excessive glow, no fake
reflections, no weak contact shadows, and no decorative geometry without purpose.
Create a bold 3D identity visual for [BRAND].

Concept:
Translate the brand’s typography or symbol into an inflated physical object with
soft pressure, believable seams, surface tension, and controlled deformation.

Composition:
Use a close crop, oversized scale, and one unexpected interaction between the
inflated form and the environment.

Materials:
Soft vinyl, translucent rubber, coated fabric, or glossy polymer.

Lighting:
Large soft source, subtle rim light, realistic folds, contact shadows, and material
highlights that reveal shape.

Typography:
Keep supporting information flat and sharply aligned so it contrasts with the
volumetric object.

Quality controls:
Avoid toy-like proportions, random balloon shapes, plastic-looking surfaces,
weak seams, duplicated forms, and unreadable type.
Create an experimental 3D product campaign for [PRODUCT].

Concept:
Deconstruct the product into carefully separated layers, components, textures,
and structural systems while preserving a clear visual hierarchy.

Composition:
Arrange the components in a controlled exploded-view layout with depth, rhythm,
and one clear visual axis.

Materials:
Show accurate material differences between metal, glass, polymer, textile,
rubber, or internal components.

Lighting:
Use precise technical lighting with soft environmental shadows and consistent
reflections across every separated part.

Typography:
Use compact annotations, one large headline, and minimal technical information.

Quality controls:
No random fragmentation, no inconsistent scale, no floating parts without logic,
no fake engineering detail, and no cluttered labels.
Create a 3D campaign environment built around the word “[WORD].”

Concept:
The typography becomes architecture. The subject or product moves through,
rests against, or is partially hidden by the letterforms.

Composition:
Use dramatic scale, strong depth, and an intentional camera angle. Keep the word
readable despite cropping and spatial interaction.

Materials:
Choose one coherent material system such as concrete, translucent acrylic,
brushed metal, foam, glass, or inflated fabric.

Lighting:
Use environmental light that reveals the depth and material of the letters while
preserving a clear focal point.

Quality controls:
No cheap extruded text, no bevel-heavy lettering, no excessive shadows,
no random perspective, and no subject that appears pasted into the scene.
Design a motion-ready 3D campaign system for [BRAND OR PRODUCT].

Concept:
Create a visual that can naturally evolve across several frames through rotation,
assembly, transformation, compression, or material change.

Frame logic:
Frame 1 introduces the core object.
Frame 2 reveals its internal system.
Frame 3 transforms its material or structure.
Frame 4 resolves into the campaign message.

Art direction:
Maintain one camera system, one lighting logic, one color palette, and one
typographic grid across all frames.

Quality controls:
No disconnected transitions, no random object changes, no inconsistent scale,
no lighting shifts without motivation, and no visual effects that weaken the
brand idea.
